About Me

Engineer. Problem solver. Builder.

I'm a mechanical engineer with 5 years of experience spanning structural analysis, thermal systems, and mechanism design. I care deeply about the intersection of rigorous engineering analysis and products that actually get built — which means I've spent as much time on factory floors and in machine shops as I have at a computer.

My work tends to involve systems where physics get complicated: high load-to-weight structures, thermally-constrained hardware, or mechanisms that need to do something clever without extra components. I like problems that require you to understand the fundamentals before you can trust the simulation.

Before my current role I spent two years at a small aerospace supplier, which taught me how to deliver under real certification pressure. Before that, I did a research stint in a university robotics lab that gave me a deep appreciation for compliant mechanism design — a toolset I've kept using in industry ever since.
